  • Vincenzo





    • Main Cast:

      • Song Joong-Ki as Vincenzo Cassano, Jeon Yeo-Been as Hong Cha-Young, Ok Taec-Yeon as Jang Jun-Woo, Choi Hyun Wook as Moon Ji-Woong, Lee Joo-Myung as Ji Seung-Wan

    • Writing Credits:

      • Park Jae-Bum

    • Genres:

      • Comedy, Law, Crime, Drama

    • Runtime:

      • 1 hour and 25 minutes





    Vincenzo Cassano is an Italian mafia consigliere who goes back to his home country, South Korea, with a single goal: To tear down the Geumga Plaza building and claim his hidden gold. Well actually, the real owner is a Chinese tycoon who passed away but he's one of the few people who knows about it. In his attempt to take the ~treasure~ out from the building's basement, he meets an odd group of tenants. Because of this, Vincenzo's original plans change, and he somehow ends up fighting for the tenants' rights.


    “To find a true friend is like finding a treasure” —Vincenzo Cassano





    This drama had everything... Comedy, corruption, crime and revenge. Everything that I love in a drama. There's A LOT that happens in the 20, 80min episodes. So plan your binge. I didn't except to laugh as much as I did. It was great. The action scenes were amazing...I thought everyone had great chemistry which is amazing.


    In conclusion, "Vincenzo" is a must-watch for fans of Korean dramas and anyone looking for a show that is both entertaining and thought-provoking. With its unique blend of genres, standout performances, and thought-provoking themes, the show is a testament to the power of great storytelling.


    Here's a fun fact: None of the Italy scenes in this drama were filmed abroad and they're all computer graphics!


    And I am definetly recommending it!
